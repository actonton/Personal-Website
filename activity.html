What is soccer?

Football is a team sport in which two teams consisting of 11 players, whose primary objective is to score goals past the opposition’s goalkeeper while preventing them from scoring in your team’s net. There are plenty of rules in football to follow. The basic rules of football are:

  1. Match is played in two-minute halves

  2. Out field players must only use their feet, head, or chest to play the ball. Only goalkeepers can use their hands, and only in their designated area.

  3. If the ball crosses the sideline, it is thrown back by the team that was not the last to touch the ball

Benefits of playing soccer

Soccer is a great sport to build health, whether it is mentally or energetically. When you play soccer, you work your entire cardiovascular system. This means your heart and lungs will greatly benefit you. Soccer is also capable of extending your life because soccer keeps you fit and slim and as a result, you are less likely to suffer from any health-related problems. Playing Soccer is a highly effective way of improving your inner wellbeing.

Furthermore, while playing soccer, the brain will release dopamine and endorphins which makes playing soccer positively addictive. Soccer gives many people a sense of belonging. The sense of belonging is an essential part of positive self-esteem. Soccer allows you to have fun with your teammates and opponents alike.

Why Soccer?

I’ve been playing soccer since year 2 and I loved it since. Playing it, being with a team, running with the ball, passing, dribbling across the field, skilling past the opposition, and scoring is pure fun. I always have to be on my toes, running, ready for the pass. I have to be aware of everything around me and making sure that my teammate with the ball has options. It is also good exercise, both for my body and my brain. I have to be able to see everything and be one step ahead of my opposition. Lastly, winning games, especially against your club’s rival club just adds that cherry on top.
